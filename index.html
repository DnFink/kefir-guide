Milk Kefir Guide

Everything you need for daily maintenance

Daily Cycle
Weekly
Vacation
Safety
1

Strain

Pour through a plastic strainer into a bowl. Use a plastic spoon to stir.

2

Save Grains

Collect the grains from the strainer. Never rinse them with water!

3

Add Milk

Put grains in a clean jar. Add 1-2 cups of fresh milk.

4

Ferment

Cover with a cloth. Leave at room temp for 24 hours.

Note: If the kefir separates into watery layers too fast, you have too many grains.

Jar Swap

Once or twice a week, start with a fresh, soap-cleaned jar to keep things hygienic.

Thin the Grains

Remove extra grains. Give them to a friend or eat them (they are probiotic!).

How to Pause (up to 3 weeks)

  • • Put grains in fresh milk.
  • • Use a tight-fitting lid.
  • • Place the jar in the fridge.
  • • To restart: Strain, throw away that milk, and start a fresh 24h cycle.
Important: Fermenting involves live bacteria. Use common sense and prioritize hygiene.

The "Sniff Test"

Fresh kefir should smell yeasty or like sourdough. If it smells "rotten," putrid, or significantly off, discard the batch and rinse the grains thoroughly in fresh milk.

Watch for Mold

If you see fuzzy growth (black, green, or pink) on the surface, discard the entire batch and the grains to be safe.

Medical Disclaimer

This guide is for informational purposes only. Consult a healthcare professional before adding fermented foods to your diet if you have a compromised immune system or specific health concerns.
